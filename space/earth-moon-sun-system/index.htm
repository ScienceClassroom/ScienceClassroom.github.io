How are Earth’s movements used to measure time?

We measure time by the movement of the sun, moon, and stars.



What is the difference between revolution and rotation?

When an object turns around an interal axis, it is a rotation. When the object circles an external axis, it is a revolution.



Why do seasons change throughout the course of the year?

Because the side of the Earth facing the sun changes.



How do the relative positions of the Sun, the Moon, and Earth cause the Moon’s phases?

The sun's reflections cause the moon's phases.



How are tides on Earth affected by the Moon and the Sun?

The moon's gravitational pull is strong enough to make waves in the ocean.



What is the difference between a solar eclipse and a lunar eclipse?

A lunar eclipse is when the moon's light is blocked by Earth's shadow. A solar eclipse is when the moon passes between Earth and the Sun.

Glossary


Sphere

A round solid figure, or its surface, with every point on its surface equidistant from its center.



Ellipse

A elongated, closed curve with two foci; shape of Earth's orbit around the sun.



Time Zone

Any one of 24 longitudinal strips on Earth about 15 degrees wide on Earth's surface in which local time is the same.



Rotation

Spinning of Earth on it's axis; used to measure time in days.



Revolution

Motion of Earth around the sun, used to measure time in years.



Ecliptic

Path of Earth's orbit around the sun.



Solstice

Occurs twice a year (June and December) when Earth's rotational axis is the most toward, or away, from the sun.



Equinox

Occurs twice a year (March and September) when Earth's rotational axis is perpendicular to a line connecting Earth's center to the center of the sun.



Tide

The periodic rise and fall of sea levcel caused by the gravitational attraction among Earth, the moon, and the sun.



Moon Phase

Changing appearance of the moon as viewed from Earth, depending on the relative positions of the sun, the moon, and Earth.



Solar Eclipse

Occurs during the new moon, when Earth enters the moon's umbra and casts a shadow on the Earth's surface.



Lunar Eclipse

Occurs during the full moon, when the moon enters Earth's umbra and Earth casts a curved shadow on the moon's surface.



Maria

Relatively flat, dark-colored regions on the moon's surface.



Regolith

Layer of debris on the moon's surface formed by the accumulation of meteoric material.
