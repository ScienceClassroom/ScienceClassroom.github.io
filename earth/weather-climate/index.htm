Earth's Weather and Climate



Q&A


What are the layers of the atmosphere?

Exosphere, Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, and Thermosphere.



How is thermal energy transferred throughout the atmosphere?

Conduction, convection, and radiation.



How do clouds form?

Clouds form when rising air, through expansion, cools to the point where some of the water vapor molecules clump together.



What processes are involved in the water cycle?

The most important are evaporation, transpiration, condensation, precipitation, and runoff.



Why do differences in air pressure produce global winds?

Because high air pressure areas are very close to low air pressure areas.



How do air masses interact along warm and cold fronts?

The movements and collisions of fronts result in this.



What are the characteristics of severe storm systems?

Precipitation and hail, to name two.



What factors determine climate?

Elevation, altitude, wind patterns, topography, and geography.



What is a climate system?

An interactive system consisting of five major components, the atmosphere, the hydrosphere, the cyrosphere, the land surface, and the biosphere.
